The Game of Hog

Current score: 0 to 0

In Hog, two players alternate turns trying to reach 100 points first. On each turn, the current player chooses some number of dice to roll, up to 10. Her turn score is the sum of the dice outcomes, unless any of the dice come up a 1 (Pig out), in which case the score for her turn is only 1 point.

To spice up the game, we will play with some special rules:

  1. Hog Wild. If the sum of both players' total scores is a multiple of 7 (e.g., 14, 21, 35), then the current player rolls 4-sided dice instead of the usual 6-sided dice.
  2. Free Bacon. If a player chooses to roll zero dice, she scores one more than the sum of the digits of her opponent's score. E.g., if player 1 has 42 points, player 0 can earn 4 + 2 + 1 = 7 points by rolling zero dice.
  3. Holiday Ham. If a player's score for the turn is a prime number, then instead she scores 5 times the number of dice rolled. (Note: 1 is not prime, but 2 is.) This modification does not apply to points earned from Free Bacon.
